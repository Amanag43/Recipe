Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 3-4 tbsp milk (adjust as needed for dough consistency)
  • Oil or ghee for frying

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, and cardamom pods. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 5-7 minutes until slightly sticky. Add rose water, turn off the heat, and set aside. Make the Dough:
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, flour, and baking soda. Add ghee and mix well. Gradually add milk to form a soft, smooth dough. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes. Shape the Dough Balls:
  3. Divide the dough into small portions and roll into smooth, crack-free balls. Fry the Dough Balls:
  4. Heat oil or ghee on low to medium heat. Gently fry the balls until they turn golden brown, stirring occasionally for even coloring. Soak in Syrup:
  5. Place the fried balls into warm sugar syrup and let them soak for at least 30 minutes. Serve Warm or Chilled:

Barfi Jamun

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup khoya (mawa)
  • 2 tbsp powdered sugar
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnish

Recipe

  1. In a bowl, mix milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda. Add ghee and mix until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add milk to form a soft dough. Let it rest for 10 minutes. Shape and Fry Jamun.
  2. Divide dough into small portions and roll into smooth balls. Heat oil on low to medium heat and fry balls until golden brown. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate pan, warm the khoya until it softens. Add powdered sugar and cardamom powder, and cook for 2-3 minutes until well combined. Divide the mixture into small portions to stuff inside each fried jamun.
  4. Carefully make a small slit in each jamun and fill with the barfi mixture. Soak in Syrup: Place stuffed jamuns into the warm sugar syrup and let them soak for 20-30 minutes. Garnish and Serve:

Kanju katli

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cashew nuts (kaju)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp ghee (for greasing) Edible silver leaf (varak), optional for decoration

Recipe

  1. Prepare the Cashew Powder: Grind the cashew nuts in a blender to a fine powder. Make sure not to over-blend as it may release oil and turn into a paste.
  2. In a non-stick pan, combine sugar and water. Heat the mixture on medium flame until the sugar dissolves completely. Continue cooking until it reaches a one-string consistency (if you take a drop between your thumb and forefinger, it should form a single string when stretched).
  3. Add the cashew powder to the sugar syrup, stirring continuously to avoid lumps. Cook on low heat, stirring until the mixture thickens and starts coming together as a dough. Add cardamom powder if desired. Once the mixture leaves the sides of the pan, turn off the heat. Knead the Dough:
  4. Grease a plate with ghee, transfer the mixture, and let it cool slightly. Once it's cool enough to handle, knead the dough until smooth.
